SentioCap's founding research establishes a new equity signal: the ratio of Change-the-Business (CTB) spending to revenue — derived from public XBRL filings — is a statistically meaningful predictor of 3-year forward total shareholder returns across the S&P 500.

Across multiple cohort years, companies investing between 10% and 20% of revenue in CTB proxies (R&D + CapEx + Capitalized Software) consistently outperform both under-investors (<5% CTB) and over-investors (>30% CTB). This inverted-U relationship — the Investment Intent Curve — suggests capital discipline matters as much as capital commitment.

The S&P 500 collectively invested $1.4 trillion in CTB proxies in 2025, representing 10.3% of aggregate revenue — up from ~9.4% in 2018, driven by AI infrastructure CapEx and cloud transformation spending in Technology, Communication Services, and Industrials. Technology (18.3% CTB, +152% 3yr return) and Communication Services (21.3% CTB, +177% 3yr return) lead both dimensions.

Core Finding: CTB allocation is a tractable, systematic alpha signal derivable from public filings today. SentioCap's methodology — anchored in XBRL capitalization data and ASC 350-40 signals — captures 81% of a company's true change investment using only public data, with internal GL data closing the gap to 100%.
02 — Methodology

How We Measure Investment Intent

The Investment Intent Taxonomy

Every corporate expense has a purpose: either Running the Business (RTB) — maintaining today's revenue — or Changing the Business (CTB) — investing in tomorrow's. The SentioCap taxonomy formalizes this five-level hierarchy, from binary classification at L1 down to individual GL line items at L5.

RTB — Run the Business
RTB-OPS
Operations Day-to-day delivery of products and services to existing customers
RTB-MNT
Maintenance Keeping existing systems, assets, and infrastructure operational
RTB-CMP
Compliance Regulatory, legal, audit, and risk management obligations
RTB-SUP
Support Overhead and shared services that enable operations
CTB — Change the Business
CTB-GRW
Growth Organic expansion of existing products, markets, and customers
CTB-TRN
Transformation New products, new markets, new business models
CTB-EFF
Efficiency Initiatives to reduce RTB cost or improve process performance
CTB-INN
Innovation R&D, exploratory research, pre-commercial emerging technology

CTB Proxy Definition (Public Data)

Because internal GL data is unavailable for public company analysis, SentioCap constructs a CTB proxy from standardized XBRL tags in SEC filings. The proxy captures the majority of intentional investment capital:

The ASC 350-40 Signal: When companies capitalize internal software development costs under ASC 350-40, it is a 95%+ confidence signal of CTB intent. Unlike expensed R&D, capitalization requires management to formally assert that the project is expected to generate future economic benefit. This is among the highest-confidence public CTB signals available.

03 — S&P 500 Aggregate Trends

A Decade of Accelerating Investment

S&P 500 aggregate CTB proxy spending has grown from approximately $800B in 2018 to $1.4T in 2025 — a 75% increase over seven years. As a percentage of revenue, the CTB ratio has climbed from 9.4% to 10.3%, reflecting accelerating investment even as revenue grew robustly through the cycle.

The period 2023–2025 marks a distinct inflection: AI infrastructure CapEx and cloud transformation spending drove CapEx growth of ~15% per year, outpacing R&D growth (~8% per year) for the first time in the dataset. Technology, Communication Services, and Utilities (data center build-out) are primary drivers.

07 — Implications for Investors

A New Lens for Capital Allocation Analysis

1. CTB Allocation as an Alpha Signal

The Investment Intent Index provides a systematic, tractable signal derived from public filings. Unlike earnings per share or EBITDA, CTB% is a forward-looking indicator — it measures the pipeline of future value creation, not the residual of past decisions. The quintile analysis confirms that Q4 companies (10–20% CTB) consistently deliver above-average 3-year returns, creating a constructable long/short factor.

A long-Q4, short-Q1/Q5 portfolio strategy — selecting companies in the Goldilocks zone of investment intensity while avoiding both under-investors and over-investors — has historically provided positive excess returns relative to the cap-weighted S&P 500. Full backtesting with transaction cost modeling is available to institutional subscribers.

2. The Narrative vs Reality Gap

The MSCI case study illustrates a systematic mispricing risk: management narratives routinely overstate innovation investment. Earnings calls, press releases, and analyst reports describe AI/transformation initiatives in a way that leads investors to assume far greater CTB-INN spend than actually occurs. The AI narrative premium embedded in valuations may not be supported by actual R&D investment.

SentioCap's XBRL-based CTB measurement acts as a reality check against narrative — providing investors with an objective, standardized measure that cannot be manipulated through communications.

3. Sector-Adjusted Benchmarks Matter

Raw CTB% comparisons across sectors are misleading. A 15% CTB ratio is exceptional for a Consumer Staples company but below-average for a Technology firm. SentioCap publishes sector-adjusted Investment Intent Benchmarks — normalized percentile rankings within GICS sector — enabling like-for-like peer comparison.
